# Large Language Models

**Category:** General
**Definition:** Large Language Models (LLMs) are deep learning models trained on vast text corpora to understand, generate, and manipulate human language at scale.

## Overview

Large Language Models are neural networks with billions to hundreds of billions of parameters that learn patterns, relationships, and representations from massive datasets of text. These models leverage transformer architectures to process and generate text, enabling capabilities ranging from text completion and translation to complex reasoning and code generation. The models acquire emergent abilities through scale, exhibiting surprising competencies not present in smaller counterparts.
