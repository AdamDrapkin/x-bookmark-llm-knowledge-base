# First Principles Thinking

**Category:** Thinking Technique
**Definition:** A reasoning approach that deconstructs problems into their most fundamental, self-evident truths before building reasoning and solutions from the ground up.

## Overview

First principles thinking is a foundational analytical method that originated in ancient Greek philosophy, where Aristotle used the term "first principles" (archê) to describe the self-evident truths that form the basis of any logical system. Rather than reasoning by analogy or relying on conventional wisdom, this approach requires breaking down any complex problem or belief into its constituent parts until you reach irreducible facts. From these basic building blocks, one then reconstructs understanding, creating new knowledge and solutions that are grounded in logic rather than assumptions. This method has been championed by practitioners throughout history—from Socrates' dialectical questioning to Richard Feynman's revolutionary physics to Elon Musk's approach to innovation. First principles thinking involves stripping away assumptions and received wisdom to identify fundamental truths, then constructing reasoning and solutions from those foundational elements.
