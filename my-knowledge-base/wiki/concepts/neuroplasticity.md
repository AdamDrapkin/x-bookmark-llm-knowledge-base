# Neuroplasticity

**Category:** General
**Definition:** The brain's capacity to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections throughout life, allowing for adaptation, learning, and recovery from injury.

## Overview

Neuroplasticity encompasses the brain's ability to modify its structure and function in response to experience, learning, and environmental changes. This process operates at multiple levels—from individual synapses to large-scale neural networks—and underlies the brain's capacity to compensate for damage, acquire new skills, and adapt to new situations throughout the lifespan.
