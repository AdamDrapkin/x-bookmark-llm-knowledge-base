# Llm

**Category:** Technique/Tool
**Definition:** A large language model (LLM) is a deep‑learning system trained on massive text corpora to understand and generate human‑language output.

## Overview

LLMs leverage transformer architectures and massive parameter counts to capture broad linguistic patterns, enabling tasks ranging from text completion to reasoning. Recent workflows combine LLMs with retrieval‑augmented generation and vector‑based personal knowledge bases, allowing models to ground responses in user‑specific information. This synergy has been popularized in services that sell customized knowledge bases and in systems such as Karpathy’s personal knowledge‑base setup.
