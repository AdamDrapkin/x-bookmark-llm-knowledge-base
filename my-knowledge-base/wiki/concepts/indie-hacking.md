# Indie Hacking

**Category:** Method
**Definition:** A rapid, low‑budget approach to building, launching, and monetizing solo or micro‑startup projects by exploiting unconventional tools, platform features, or hardware capabilities.

## Overview

Indie hacking is the practice of a single developer or tiny team creating and shipping products in a very short timeframe, often with little to no external funding, and focusing on early revenue over scale. By leveraging existing APIs, device sensors, or niche platforms, indie hackers can prototype and validate ideas in days rather than months, iterating based on real‑world feedback. This approach prioritizes speed, minimal viable products, and direct monetization strategies such as one‑time sales, freemium upgrades, or affiliate links.
