# Multi Agent Systems

**Category:** General
**Definition:** A multi‑agent system (MAS) is a computerized system composed of multiple interacting autonomous agents that perceive their environment, reason, and act to achieve individual or collective goals.

## Overview

Multi‑agent systems are a branch of artificial intelligence and distributed computing that models complex behavior through the interaction of many autonomous entities called agents. Each agent possesses its own goals, knowledge, and decision‑making capabilities, and they can cooperate, coordinate, or compete to solve problems that are beyond the abilities of a single agent. MAS are employed in diverse fields such as robotics, traffic control, resource management, and simulations of social phenomena. The primary purpose of a multi‑agent system is to leverage collective intelligence, coordination, and robustness of multiple autonomous agents to address complex, distributed problems.
