# Indie Hacker

**Category:** General
**Definition:** An individual who builds and monetizes personal projects, startups, or small businesses independently, often employing bootstrapping, automation, and community collaboration.

## Overview

The indie hacker movement consists of entrepreneurs who create small, revenue‑focused online businesses with little or no external funding, leveraging existing platforms and tools to launch quickly. They prioritize rapid iteration, data‑driven decision making, and transparent sharing of progress to attract users and early revenue. The culture values autonomy, personal branding, and sustainable growth over traditional venture‑capital funding. An indie hacker is a solo or small‑team entrepreneur who builds and monetizes self‑funded online projects, often sharing their journey publicly and aiming for quick, sustainable profitability.
