# Zettelkasten

**Category:** Method
**Definition:** A personal knowledge management system using interconnected atomic notes to capture, develop, and retrieve ideas.

## Overview

Zettelkasten (German for "slip box") is a note-taking and knowledge management methodology developed by sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who used it to write over 70 books and 400 articles. The system emphasizes writing ideas as atomic, self-contained notes that are linked through explicit connections rather than organized hierarchically. This approach transforms notes into a dynamic network of knowledge that grows organically and enables emergent insights through cross-referencing.
