# Y Combinator

**Type:** Company
**Known For:** Providing seed funding, mentorship, and a three‑month accelerator program to early‑stage startups; credited with launching many successful tech firms such as Dropbox, Airbnb, and Reddit.

## Overview

Y Combinator (YC) is a startup accelerator founded in 2005 by Paul Graham, Jessica Livingston, Robert Morris, and Trevor Blackwell, headquartered in Mountain View, California. Twice a year it selects a cohort of early‑stage startups, offering each a modest amount of capital, intensive mentorship, and access to a vast alumni network in exchange for a small equity stake (typically about 7%). Over the past two decades, YC has become one of the most influential forces in the technology ecosystem, with its alumni raising billions in subsequent funding and achieving numerous high‑profile exits. Y Combinator is a leading accelerator that mentors and funds early‑stage startups, profoundly shaping the modern tech landscape through its rigorous program and extensive alumni network.
