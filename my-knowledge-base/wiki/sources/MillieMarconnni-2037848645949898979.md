# Garry Tan's AI-Assisted Code Revolution: 10,000-20,000 Lines Per Day

**Author:** @MillieMarconnni
**Category:** philosophy
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Type:** bookmark
**Source:** [Tweet](https://x.com/MillieMarconnni/status/2037848645949898979)

## Summary

This tweet marks what appears to be a watershed moment in the understanding of individual productivity in the AI era. Garry Tan, President & CEO of [[Y Combinator]]—the accelerator that funded [[Coinbase]], [[Instacart]], [[Rippling]], and thousands of other companies—reveals that he is personally shipping 10,000 to 20,000 lines of production code per day, part-time, while running the most powerful startup accelerator in the world.

The underlying document (gstack README) provides the full context. Tan opens with a striking quote from [[Andrej Karpathy]]: "I don't think I've typed like a line of code probably since December, basically, which is an extremely large change." Tan then draws a direct parallel to [[Peter Steinberger]], who built [[OpenClaw]]—247,000 GitHub stars—essentially solo with AI agents. The core thesis: "The revolution is here. A single builder with the right tooling can move faster than a traditional team."

The numbers are staggering. In the last 60 days, Tan shipped 600,000+ lines of production code (35% tests), with peak days reaching 10,000-20,000 lines. His weekly retrospective shows 140,751 lines added, 362 commits, and approximately 115,000 net lines of code. Compare this to 2013, when he built [[Bookface]], YC's internal social network—that entire year yielded only 772 contributions. In 2026, he's already at 1,237 contributions and counting.

Tan frames this as a twenty-year arc: "I've been building products for twenty years, and right now I'm shipping more code than I ever have." The key qualifier is tooling—"gstack is my answer"—suggesting the leverage comes not just from AI but from a carefully constructed personal development environment.

The philosophical weight is significant. Tan represents the gatekeeper of startup funding for an entire generation. If he is demonstrating that a single person with AI can now operate at the scale of a twenty-person team, this has profound implications for venture-backed startup economics, the meaning of "team," and what it means to be a technical founder versus an AI-augmented builder.

## Key Takeaways

- A single [[AI agent|augmented developer]] can now output code at a rate previously requiring twenty people, fundamentally changing startup economics
- The key to extreme productivity is not just AI but a carefully crafted tooling stack ("gstack is my answer")
- [[Y Combinator]]'s CEO believes the era of solo-built, AI-assisted products at massive scale has arrived
- [[Andrej Karpathy]] has reportedly not typed a line of code since December 2025, representing a new paradigm
- Peter Steinberger's OpenClaw (247K stars) is cited as proof that individual developers can build infrastructure used by millions
- Code quality metrics (35% tests) suggest this isn't just raw output but sustainable development
