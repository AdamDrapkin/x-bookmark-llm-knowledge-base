# Eyeballs Method: Early Adoption of Mass Content Generation

**Author:** @JamesEbringer

## Summary

James Ebringer reflects on the progression of [[ai-powered-content-automation]] strategies within the social media and digital marketing space. In this tweet, he observes that many practitioners are only now "discovering" the power of mass content generation and mass posting—techniques he claims to have been implementing for months prior using what he calls the **Eyeballs Method**. The tweet carries a tone of vindication and "early mover" legitimacy, positioning Ebringer as ahead of the curve.

Crucially, Ebringer anchors his timeline to **September 2024**, emphasizing that he developed and deployed his approach during a period when key tools in the current AI agent ecosystem—including [[openclaw]] and capable [[ai-image-generation]] tools—did not yet exist. This suggests his methodology either relied on different tooling, more manual processes, or earlier/lesser-known AI capabilities. The tweet serves as both a humble brag and a commentary on the democratization of [[mass-posting]] techniques, noting that what's now being discovered and adopted by a wider audience has been refined in relative obscurity.

The underlying observation is that the techniques of volume-based content creation—generating and distributing large quantities of posts, media, and messaging at scale—are transitioning from niche practitioner knowledge to mainstream awareness.

## Key Takeaways

- The Eyeballs Method represents an early, systematic approach to mass content generation and posting
- Ebringer claims to have been operating since September 2024, prior to the availability of popular tools like OpenClaw
- The tweet suggests the approach worked even with limited AI tooling available at that time
- A broader trend is emerging: mass posting and automated content generation techniques are gaining wider adoption
- Early practitioners who built systems before tool proliferation may hold advantages in process refinement
