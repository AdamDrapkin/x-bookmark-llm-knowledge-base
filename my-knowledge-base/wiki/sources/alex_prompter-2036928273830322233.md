# Janusian Thinking Prompt for Creative Blocks

**Author:** @alex_prompter
**Posted:** March 25, 2026
**Source:** [Quote Tweet](https://x.com/alex_prompter/status/2036928273830322233)

## Summary

[[janusian-thinking]] is a cognitive technique named after Janus, the Roman deity depicted with two faces looking in opposite directions. The core principle challenges the assumption that creative thinking must follow linear, logical progressions. Instead, it proposes that holding two **contradictory opinions simultaneously** can unlock breakthroughs that neither single perspective could achieve alone.

In this tweet, @alex_prompter offers a reusable [[prompt-template]] for applying Janusian thinking to any creative block. The technique reframes cognitive dissonance from a problem to be resolved into a tool for exploration. By deliberately entertaining mutually exclusive ideas, practitioners can discover unexpected angles, challenge assumptions, and generate novel solutions.

The tweet references two linked resources (indicated by t.co URLs) that likely expand on the underlying psychological research and practical applications of this approach.

## Key Takeaways

- **Contradiction as fuel:** Accepting that two opposing ideas can both be "true" in a given context creates cognitive space for innovation
- **Breaks linear patterns:** Most people default to straight-line thinking; Janusian thinking deliberately introduces productive tension
- **Universal applicability:** The prompt is framed as adaptable to "any creative block," suggesting broad utility across domains
- **Structured prompt approach:** Rather than a vague concept, @alex_prompter provides an implementable framework (via the "JANUSIAN THINKER" prompt block)
- **Psychological foundation:** The technique draws from established cognitive science research on [[divergent-thinking]] and [[lateral-thinking]]
