## Summary

This tweet captures a fundamental shift in how [[senior-engineer|experienced engineers]] approach their work when leveraging AI coding tools like [[claude-code]]. The core insight challenges the common misconception that AI simply makes you type code faster. Instead, the real transformation is in *where* you spend your time and cognitive energy.

The engineer is pointing out that with effective AI assistance, the traditional "write code" activity shrinks dramatically. Instead, your time shifts toward [[prompt-crafting|writing detailed prompts]], [[code-review|reviewing AI-generated code]], and [[system-architecture|architectural planning]]. This represents a genuine leverage multiplier—each hour of human effort can produce significantly more value because the mechanical work of typing is delegated while the strategic thinking remains human.

The tweet implies a deliberate, intentional workflow where the engineer has structured their process to maximize this shift. By investing more effort upfront in crafting detailed instructions and downstream in thorough review, they can scale their output far beyond what pure typing speed would allow.

## Key Takeaways

- AI coding tools change *where* you work, not just *how fast* you work
- Time investment shifts from typing to [[prompt-engineering]], planning, and review
- The leverage comes from [[automation|automating]] routine coding while focusing human effort on judgment and strategy
- Effective use requires more detailed prompts, not less thinking
- This represents a fundamental change in the [[engineering-productivity]] model
