# Claude Context Window Token Waste Problem

**Author:** @ArchiveExplorer
**Category:** pricing-costs
**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Type:** quote_tweet
**Source:** [Tweet](https://x.com/ArchiveExplorer/status/2040727747966668939)

## Summary

@ArchiveExplorer expresses frustration with repeatedly hitting Claude's daily usage limits—experiencing the "Claude usage limit reached" notification "every fucking day." They were on the verge of upgrading to the $200/month Max plan to solve the problem. However, after reading an article (self-referenced via their profile links), they discovered that 98.5% of tokens in typical usage are being wasted. The core revelation: users aren't paying for answers—they're paying for Claude to re-read its own conversation history 30+ times throughout a session due to how context windows function.

This reframes the entire pricing problem. The daily limits aren't a sign of heavy usage; they're a symptom of extreme inefficiency in how [[Claude]] processes conversation context. The article apparently detailed how [[context window]] management causes the model to repeatedly tokenize and process the entire conversation history, rather than efficiently maintaining state. This waste compounds quickly, especially in longer conversations or when using Claude for ongoing projects.

After discovering this inefficiency, @ArchiveExplorer reconsidered the Max upgrade, suggesting that the real solution isn't paying more for higher limits—it's addressing the underlying token waste problem.

## Key Takeaways

- Claude's usage limits reset daily, but users on active projects hit them repeatedly
- The $200/month Max subscription appeared to be the solution, but revealed token waste changes the calculus
- An estimated 98.5% of tokens in typical usage are "wasted" through redundant context re-processing
- The model allegedly "re-reads its own homework" (conversation history) approximately 30 times per session
- Token inefficiency, not actual compute-heavy tasks, drives most usage limit consumption
- This affects users running Claude on ongoing projects far more than one-off queries
