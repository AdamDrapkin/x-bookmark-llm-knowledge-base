## Summary

Kanika presents Greg Isenberg's free masterclass on seven distribution strategies that work for building revenue from AI-created products. The central thesis challenges the conventional wisdom that building a great product first will attract customers. Instead, Kanika argues that because [[ai]] has democratized [[vibecoding]] and building, distribution and marketing skills have become the most valuable asset in tech. The formula presented—**10,000 pages × 30 visits × 2% conversion × $10 per sale = $60K/month**—illustrates the leverage available when you focus on distribution over endless feature building.

The video dives deep into a philosophical shift: when Kanika moved to Silicon Valley in 2014, engineers ruled the hierarchy, followed by product managers, with marketers as the "laughingstock." Today, she argues the hierarchy has inverted—**distribution-first thinkers sit at the top**, then product people, then developers. This matters because AI can now build anything, but getting customers to that thing remains the unsolved problem. With 200,000 new vibe-coded projects created daily on platforms like [[lovable]], most will fade into obscurity without a distribution strategy.

Kanika uses [[peter-lovells]] as the prime example: $3M+ in revenue, zero employees, no VC funding. His success stems from three things—**a massive audience (750,000+ followers), consistent content creation (125,000 tweets over 8 years), and strong [[seo]]**. The core framework she advocates: build an audience first (even just 1,000 people), ask them what pain points they have, build the solution in 24-72 hours, then launch to a warm audience that already trusts you. This "distribution first, product second, always" approach cuts through the silence that plagues most builders.

## Key Takeaways

- **The Great Hierarchy Flip:** [[ai]] has inverted the Silicon Valley hierarchy—marketers and distribution experts now hold more value than engineers because building is commoditized
- **Distribution First, Product Second:** Start with audience growth, validate needs through conversation, build fast, launch warm—this inverts the traditional "build and they will come" failure loop
- **Peter Lovells Blueprint:** Zero employees, $3M+ revenue, achieved through audience consistency (125,000 tweets), trust-building, and SEO—not superior technology
- **Volume Math for Passive Income:** 10K pages generating 30 visits each with 2% conversion at $10 = $60K/month—the leverage is in distribution infrastructure, not product complexity
- **MCP Servers as Sales Team:** [[mcp-servers]] can be positioned as automated sales infrastructure, discovered by AI assistants and delivering solutions directly to users
- **200K Daily Projects Need Customers:** With 200,000 new vibe-coded projects spawning daily on platforms like [[lovable]], distribution skills are the rare differentiator
