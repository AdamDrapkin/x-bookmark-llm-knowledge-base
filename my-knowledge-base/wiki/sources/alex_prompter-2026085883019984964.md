## Summary

This tweet introduces a [[prompt-engineering]] approach to cognitive reframing through an "Optimism Rewiring Coach" tool. The author leverages [[harvard-medical-school]] research to establish credibility, citing compelling statistics: optimists experience an 11-15% increase in longevity and demonstrate 35% lower cardiovascular risk. Notably, these benefits persist even when controlling for confounders like diet, exercise, and socioeconomic status—suggesting the optimism effect operates somewhat independently of traditional health behaviors.

The tweet frames optimism as a trainable cognitive skill rather than a fixed personality trait, implying [[neuroplasticity]]-based interventions can shift baseline outlook. This positions the "Optimism Rewiring Coach" as a practical application of [[positive-psychology]] research translated into a [[gpt]] or [[ai-assistant]] format.
