## Summary

This tweet makes a provocative claim — that formal education teaches memorization rather than [[critical-thinking]] — and offers 6 Claude prompts as a free alternative to a $100,000 degree. The hook is the contrast between the perceived failure of higher education and the promise of AI-assisted learning. However, the image attached to the tweet does not actually display the 6 promised prompts. Instead, it shows a welcome screen from what appears to be a [[sonnet-4-6]] AI assistant interface, greeting the user as "God" and presenting categorized interaction options: Write, Learn, Code, Life stuff, and a Google Drive integration.

This creates a notable gap between the tweet's bold assertion and the evidence provided. The image depicts an [[ai-assistant-interface]] rather than the specific prompt templates the caption promises. The "God" greeting is a common [[persona-based-prompt]] technique — assigning the AI an elevated identity to alter its tone and engagement level — but the actual mechanism behind this greeting is not explained. The categorization of tasks (Write, Learn, Code, Life stuff) reflects the broader trend of [[model-integration]] with productivity tools like Google Drive, suggesting the model is intended as a unified workspace assistant rather than a specialized thinking coach.

The disconnect between the tweet's promise and the image's content raises questions about whether this is a deliberate bait-and-switch to drive engagement, or simply a misattributed visual. Either way, it illustrates how [[prompt-engineering]] claims spread virally with minimal substantiation.
