## Summary

This tweet highlights a groundbreaking open-source tool called `legal-assistant` that leverages [[claude-code]] and multi-agent architecture to perform professional-grade contract review in under 60 seconds. The tool deploys 5 AI agents working in parallel to analyze contracts, flag risks, check compliance, negotiate terms, and generate legal documents—including NDAs and client-ready PDF reports. The tweet emphasizes the stark cost difference: while a lawyer typically charges $300-500 per hour for contract review, this tool can accomplish the same task nearly instantaneously. The capabilities include commands like `/legal review`, `/legal risks`, `/legal plain` (simplified language), `/legal negotiate`, `/legal compare`, `/legal compliance`, `/legal nda`, `/legal terms`, and `/legal report-pdf`. The tool specifically targets freelancers (82% don't read contracts) and small businesses (67% lack legal review), addressing the $10,000+ cost of a single bad clause.

## Key Takeaways

- **Speed:** Full contract review completed in under 60 seconds vs. hours for human lawyers
- **Cost Efficiency:** Dramatically reduces the $1,500-3,000 baseline cost for basic contract reviews
- **Multi-Agent Architecture:** 5 AI agents working in parallel for comprehensive analysis
- **Comprehensive Features:** 14 skills covering review, risk assessment, plain language conversion, negotiation, compliance checking, and document generation
- **Target Users:** Freelancers and small businesses who typically lack access to legal expertise
- **Output:** Professional PDF reports suitable for client delivery
- **Open Source:** Available on GitHub for community use and contribution
