# Gemini 3 UI Design Prompting Tutorial by Meng To

**Author:** @MengTo
**Category:** design
**Date:** November 20, 2025
**Type:** tutorial
**Source:** [Tweet](https://x.com/MengTo/status/1991489885690364187)
**Duration:** 37 minutes

## Summary

Meng To, known for his design work and resources, recorded an extensive tutorial demonstrating how to leverage [[Gemini 3]] for generating professional UI designs. The core argument: while initial prompts often produce generic, purple-tinted, overly-rounded interfaces, Gemini 3 can generate exceptional results when prompted correctly—comparable to the transformative moment when [[Midjourney]] changed digital art.

The tutorial centers on three techniques: (1) **feeding inspiration** through screenshots from [[Mobbin]], [[Dribbble]], and curated collections to establish style benchmarks; (2) **editing and enhancing** outputs using tools like [[Unicorn Studio]] for animations and [[Aura]] for templates; and (3) **exporting to production workflows** via [[Cursor]], [[Lovable]], or [[Figma]] for further refinement. Meng emphasizes specificity over generic requests—asking for "hero section" or "pricing section" rather than "landing page" yields dramatically better results.

## Key Takeaways

- **Style references are non-negotiable**: Simply specifying "in the style of Apple" or feeding actual screenshots eliminates generic purple/blue color palettes and forces proper visual hierarchy
- **Specificity beats generality**: Break designs into sections (hero, features, pricing) rather than requesting complete landing pages at once
- **Animations are Gemini 3's strength**: Unlike [[ChatGPT 5.1]], Gemini 3 handles interactions, border beams, scroll animations, and complex transitions well
- **Non-rounded corners are now possible**: Traditional AI design tools default to rounded corners; Gemini 3 respects geometric preferences when specified
- **Light mode generation has improved**: Previously weak at light themes, Gemini 3 now produces excellent light and dark variants
- **Remixing combines creativity**: Gemini 3 can blend multiple designs into novel outputs—taking one section from design A and combining it with another section from design B
- **Cursor for production**: Meng prefers [[Cursor]] for its granular control when iterating on generated code

## Core Techniques

### 1. Inspiration Feeding

The most critical technique involves building a **reference library** of premium designs. Meng recommends:

- [[Mobbin]] for organized UI patterns with animations and section breakdowns
- [[MyMind]] for curating saved inspirations
- [[Dribbble]] and [[Behance]] for style references

The workflow: collect screenshots → feed them into [[Gemini 3]] → specify "adapt" to modify text, colors, and elements while preserving layout quality.

### 2. Editing and Animation

Gemini 3 excels at adding animations to static designs. Key prompts include:
- "Add border beam animation on hover"
- "Add scroll-triggered reveals"
- Using [[Unicorn Studio]] to inject motion into layouts

This is where [[ChatGPT 5.1]] struggles—the tutorial explicitly contrasts Gemini 3's animation capabilities against competitor limitations.

### 3. Export Integration

Outputs (HTML with Tailwind/CSS) can be exported to:
- [[Lovable]] for rapid prototyping
- [[Figma]] for design handoff
- [[Cursor]] for code-level refinement

## Critique and Context

The tutorial presents an optimistic view of AI design generation. Key limitations to consider:

- Generative outputs still require significant human curation
- Reference feeding raises intellectual property questions
- Animation quality varies significantly based on prompt specificity
- Light mode generation improvements may not generalize to all design systems
