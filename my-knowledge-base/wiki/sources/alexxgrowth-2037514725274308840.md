# Creator Journey: From $0 to $6,800/mo in 2 Months

**Author:** @alexxgrowth
**Category:** content-creation
**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Type:** bookmark
**Source:** [Tweet](https://x.com/alexxgrowth/status/2037514725274308840)

## Summary

In this post, @alexxgrowth shares a conversation with a creator who achieved remarkable momentum — going from zero revenue to $6,800 per month within just two months. When prompted about what they'd change if starting over, the creator's response cuts against the grain of common advice: they wouldn't spend time learning. Instead, they'd begin posting immediately.

The creator disclosed they lost an entire week to watching YouTube tutorials about content creation techniques, thumbnail design, and growth strategies — time that could have been spent actually publishing and iterating. This anecdote illustrates a widespread behavioral pattern in the creator economy where consuming educational content masquerades as productive work. The distinction matters: while tutorials and guides have value, they can become a form of [procrastination](/procrastination) when they replace action rather than supplement it. The creator's trajectory suggests that early iteration and audience feedback provide more learning than passive consumption ever could.

## Key Takeaways

- Rapid monetization ($0 to $6,800/mo in 2 months) is achievable with decisive action rather than extended preparation
- Consuming tutorials can create a false sense of productivity — learning without applying is still delay
- The first week of a creator's journey is particularly vulnerable to tutorial rabbit holes
- Real-world feedback from posting beats theoretical knowledge from videos
- Iteration and quantity of output appear more valuable than perfecting craft beforehand for early-stage creators
