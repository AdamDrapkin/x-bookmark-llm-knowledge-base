# Paper Snapshot Feature Announcement

**Author:** @stephenhaney
**Category:** web-development
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Type:** announcement
**Source:** [Tweet](https://x.com/stephenhaney/status/2036485780961669214)

## Summary

Stephen Haney announced Paper Snapshot, a new feature for the Paper design tool that enables users to capture their live website and paste it directly into Paper as fully editable layers. This represents a significant workflow improvement for designers and developers who previously had to rely on static screenshots when prototyping or iterating on existing sites.

The key innovation of Paper Snapshot is that it preserves the actual [[html]] and [[css]] structure from the captured website rather than flattening it into an image. This means designers can start from real, production sites and maintain editability of individual elements. The feature bridges the gap between live web development and design prototyping, allowing teams to quickly iterate on existing designs without manually recreating layouts.

## Key Takeaways

- Enables capturing live websites as editable layers in Paper
- Preserves real HTML/CSS structure rather than flattening to images
- Eliminates the need for manual screenshot imports and trace-overs
- Allows designers to start prototypes directly from production sites
- Maintains editability of individual elements and layers
