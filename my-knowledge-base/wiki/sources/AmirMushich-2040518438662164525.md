## Summary

In this thread opener, @AmirMushich challenges the AI design industry's fixation on pricing metrics ("how much") while neglecting the fundamental question of motivation ("why"). Drawing on a decade of industry experience, the author argues a contrarian position: **businesses don't actually purchase design — they purchase cost savings on design**. The tweet begins to enumerate three value propositions businesses seek from visual services, though the third point and the full explanation of the three categories appear truncated in the provided text. The tweet includes a McDonald's branded promotional image featuring a woman with a drink and paper bag, suggesting commercial visual work as the context for this commentary.

## Key Takeaways

- The AI design community focuses too heavily on pricing mechanics rather than understanding client psychology
- Businesses fundamentally seek **economic efficiency** — reducing costs associated with visual content production
- "Design" is not the purchase; **the outcome or cost reduction** is what businesses actually buy
- The author implies that designers and AI tool providers misunderstand their value proposition in the eyes of clients
- Industry conversations should shift from "what should I charge?" to "what cost am I eliminating for my client?"
- A decade of experience suggests this fundamental misalignment is widespread in the space
