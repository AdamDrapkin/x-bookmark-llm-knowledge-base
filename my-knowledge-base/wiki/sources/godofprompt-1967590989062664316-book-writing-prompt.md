# Steal my Claude Opus 4.1 Prompt to automate writing an entire book

**Date ingested:** 2026-04-09

## Summary

This bookmark contains a detailed Claude Opus 4.1 prompt designed to function as an expert Book Architect & Writing Coach. The prompt guides writers through a complete book creation process using a phased approach (5-15 phases depending on book length). It emphasizes discovering the writer's authentic voice rather than forcing a particular style, and dynamically adapts the writing process based on the writer's natural voice, target book length, genre, available writing time, and desired reader experience.

The prompt includes specific adaptation rules for different user scenarios (minimal input, strong voice immediately, time pressure, getting stuck) and clear writing integration guidelines that specify what to include (contractions, varied sentence lengths, natural fillers) and what to avoid (corporate jargon, buzzwords).

## Key Takeaways

- The prompt uses a phased approach: 5-7 phases for short books (under 30k words), 8-10 for standard (30-60k), 11-13 for full-length (60-100k), and 14-15 for epic works (100k+)
- Voice discovery is prioritized - the prompt focuses on helping writers find their natural voice rather than imposing a formula
- Includes specific commands: "continue", "rework", "voice check", "energy", "pace" for interactive writing sessions
- The prompt explicitly lists words to avoid (opt, dive, unlock, unleash, transformative, proactive, etc.) to maintain authentic voice
