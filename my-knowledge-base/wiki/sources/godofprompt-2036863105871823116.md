## Summary

This prompt, shared by @godofprompt, establishes a meta-framework for interacting with Claude by repositioning it as a strategic CEO-level advisor rather than a task executor. The core technique involves three foundational elements: positioning the AI as a "second brain" (a concept popularized by [[Tiago Forte]]'s [[Building a Second Brain]] methodology), establishing systems thinking as the default cognitive mode instead of task-oriented processing, and anchoring the AI's perspective at the highest level of abstraction ("top of the mountain").

The visual accompanying the tweet features three iconic tech CEOs—[[Elon Musk]], [[Steve Jobs]], and [[Bill Gates]]—serving as symbolic anchors that reinforce the authority and scope of thinking the prompt aims to evoke. The technique is fundamentally about [[reframing]] the AI's role: rather than responding to isolated queries, it should operate as an integrated thinking partner that considers cascading implications across the entire organization or life context. The prompt text is partially truncated in the original tweet, cutting off at what appears to be a phrase about moving away from task-level thinking toward more holistic strategic reasoning.

## Key Takeaways

- **Persona anchoring** is a powerful prompt technique—establishing a clear identity (CEO, strategic advisor) shapes the entire output style, depth, and framing of responses
- The "second brain" framing creates an expectation of **integrated, recall-ready knowledge** rather than isolated answers
- "Think in systems, not tasks" is a concise but profound **cognitive directive** that shifts AI behavior from reactive to proactive, strategic reasoning
- **Perspective elevation** ("top of the mountain") signals to the AI that it should consider the highest-order implications first before diving into details
- The visual metaphor of legendary CEOs serves as **social proof** and sets user expectations for the quality and ambition of the AI's responses
- This type of meta-prompt works best when followed by specific questions or contexts—the CEO persona provides the lens, but the user must still provide the strategic challenge
