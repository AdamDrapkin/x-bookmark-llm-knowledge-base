## Summary

This tweet shares a [[prompt-engineering]] technique derived from [[NTU]] Singapore research on [[Chain-of-Verification]] (CoV). The core insight is that CoV—a method originally designed to reduce [[hallucination]]s in LLM outputs—has a secondary benefit: it significantly boosts [[divergent thinking]], a key component of creative problem-solving. The researcher claims this technique makes AI approximately 12% more creative by forcing the model to systematically interrogate, verify, and expand upon its own initial responses rather than accepting the first answer at face value.

The mechanism works by transforming the typical single-pass generation into a multi-stage dialogue with the model itself. After generating an initial response, the prompt instructs the AI to question its own assumptions, identify potential gaps, and generate alternative perspectives. This self-reflection loop creates what researchers call a "verification phase" that opens up the solution space beyond the model's default response patterns.

The tweet references research backing this approach, suggesting the findings have been peer-reviewed at NTU, though the specific paper is not named in the original post. The attached image presumably contains the actual prompt template that implements this technique in practice.
