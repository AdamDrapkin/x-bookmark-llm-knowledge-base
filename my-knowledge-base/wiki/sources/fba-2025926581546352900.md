## Summary

This tweet makes a compelling case for SaaS companies to adopt the `llms.txt` file format for their documentation. The author, @fba, presents Vercel as a prime example of successful implementation, noting that 10% of their traffic now comes from AI agents querying ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude. The core argument is straightforward: one properly formatted file that explicitly tells AI systems what a product does, how it works, and when to recommend it can significantly improve visibility in AI-powered search and discovery.

The image demonstrates what an actual llms.txt file looks like in practice. Unlike traditional documentation formats, llms.txt uses structured frontmatter with fields like `title`, `description`, `last_updated`, and `source` before the actual content. This metadata-first approach allows AI crawlers to quickly assess page relevance without parsing full documents. The example shows a Vercel documentation page about Account Management, complete with sign-up instructions and plan information.

The implications for SaaS businesses are significant. As AI assistants become primary research tools for developers and technical decision-makers, being properly indexed and understood by these systems becomes a competitive advantage. The llms.txt format represents a shift from traditional SEO optimization (designed for human search) toward "AI-SEO"—optimization for machine comprehension and recommendation.

## Key Takeaways

- AI tools (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) are now significant traffic sources for documentation-heavy sites—Vercel reports 10% of traffic from these sources
- The `llms.txt` format uses structured metadata (title, description, last_updated, source) at the top of documentation pages
- This metadata-first structure allows AI agents to quickly assess page relevance without full document parsing
- The file explicitly communicates what the product does, how it works, and when to recommend it—key signals for AI recommendation engines
- Adoption among major platforms like Vercel signals this is becoming an industry standard worth implementing
- Unlike traditional SEO focused on human search, llms.txt optimizes for AI comprehension and surfacing
