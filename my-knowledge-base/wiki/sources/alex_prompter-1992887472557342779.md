## Summary

This tweet promotes a paradigm shift in market research methodology, suggesting that [[gemini-3]] can replace expensive analyst subscriptions such as [[gartner]] by generating full industry reports using public data and [[prompt-engineering]] techniques. The author presents this as a disruption to traditional [[market-research]] economics, where organizations typically pay substantial fees for synthesized industry intelligence.

The underlying concept involves structuring prompts to instruct the AI model to act as a "full-stack market research analyst" — gathering, analyzing, and synthesizing publicly available information into actionable industry reports. This approach assumes that the quality of output depends heavily on the prompt architecture, which must guide the model through data collection frameworks, analytical methodologies, and report formatting. The marketing positioning emphasizes cost elimination rather than cost reduction, framing AI-generated reports as complete substitutes for human analyst work.

## Key Takeaways

- **Cost disruption:** AI-generated market research can potentially replace subscriptions costing thousands to tens of thousands of dollars annually
- **Public data dependency:** The approach relies on [[publicly-available-data]] rather than proprietary databases, meaning report depth depends on what information is openly accessible
- **Prompt architecture matters:** The technique emphasizes crafting detailed instructions that guide the model through analytical frameworks
- **Multi-step synthesis:** Effective prompts likely instruct the model to gather data, apply analytical frameworks, compare competitors, identify trends, and format conclusions
- **Limitations awareness:** Outputs depend on the model's training data cutoff and may not include the most recent developments or proprietary research
