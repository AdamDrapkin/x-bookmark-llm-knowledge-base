## 
Step 2: Steal Design Taste From Apps You Already Use

You don't need design skills. You need references.

Every app on your phone was designed by professionals. Use that. When you're prompting in Lovable, borrow the visual language of apps you already know and love.

These phrases work incredibly well:

- "Make it feel like Linear" → You get clean, minimal, developer-tool aesthetics

- "Design it like Notion" → Clean typography, spacious layout, soft UI

- "Style it similar to Stripe's website" → Premium, polished, trustworthy

- "Give it a Spotify vibe" → Dark mode, bold colors, modern feel

- "Make it look like the Apple Store" → Lots of whitespace, hero imagery, elegant simplicity

Lovable understands these references and translates them into real design decisions: colors, spacing, typography, component styles.

You're not copying these apps. You're communicating a visual direction in five words instead of writing a design brief. That's the shortcut.

Pro tip: If you see a website or app you love, tell Lovable about it. "I really like how Calm's app looks: soft gradients, rounded buttons, peaceful color palette. Apply that aesthetic to my project." That's a completely valid prompt and it works.
